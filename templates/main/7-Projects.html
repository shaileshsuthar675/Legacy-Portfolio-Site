Research Paper



Kinematic Simulation of 6-DOF Clubfoot (CTEV) Corrective Orthosis for its Automation Feasibilities for Integrated Manufacturing


Authors: Harlal Singh Mali

Co-authors: Abhilash Bint, Shailesh Suthar, and Shamsher Singh

Manuscript Submitted in: Journal of Materials Engineering and Performance, Springer Nature, Volume 31, Issue 1

Abstract: The research addresses Congenital Talipes Equinovarus (CTEV), a pediatric foot abnormality affecting 1-8 in 1000 live births. While the Ponseti method is currently the standard non-invasive treatment, this study develops an innovative 6-Degree of Freedom (DOF) unilateral clubfoot orthosis to overcome limitations of traditional treatments. By investigating 2 and 3-DOF mechanisms and employing forward and inverse kinematics, the research aims to create an automated, sensor-integrated orthosis that can potentially revolutionize clubfoot treatment through advanced manufacturing techniques. The primary focus is on identifying foot trajectory and determining joint actuation angles to enable future smart orthosis development.
